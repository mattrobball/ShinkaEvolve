I will replace the Fermat configuration with a "Star Configuration" of 6 lines in P^2.
A star configuration of s lines consists of the set of pairwise intersection points of s linear forms in general position.
It is a known result in commutative algebra that the resurgence of the ideal of a star configuration of s lines in P^2 is exactly 2(s-1)/s.
For s=6, the resurgence is 2(5)/6 = 5/3 â‰ˆ 1.667.
This is significantly higher than the resurgence of the Fermat configuration (1.5) or the generic case (1.0).
This should produce containment failures for ratios up to ~1.67, maximizing the resurgence score.
I will define the 6 lines in general position and construct the ideal, which is generated by the products of any 5 of the 6 linear forms.
